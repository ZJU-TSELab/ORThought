Set:
1. Cities
The set of cities to visit, \( N = \{1, 2, 3, 4\} \).

Parameter:
1. DistanceMatrix
A matrix \( d_{ij} \) representing the distance from city i to city j.
[
  [0, 10, 20, 12],
  [10, 0, 5, 10],
  [20, 5, 0, 8],
  [15, 12, 8, 0]
]

Decision variable:
1. TravelArc
Binary variable, \( x_{ij} \) for \( i, j \in N, i \ne j \), is 1 if the salesman travels directly from city i to city j, and 0 otherwise.
2. SubtourElimination
Continuous variable, \( u_i \) for \( i \in N \), used for the Miller-Tucker-Zemlin (MTZ) subtour elimination formulation, representing the position of city i in the tour.

Objective:
Minimize: \( \sum_{i \in N} \sum_{j \in N, i \ne j} d_{ij} x_{ij} \)

Constraint:
1. LeaveEachCity
For each city \( i \in N \), exactly one arc must leave: \( \sum_{j \in N, j \ne i} x_{ij} = 1 \)
2. EnterEachCity
For each city \( j \in N \), exactly one arc must enter: \( \sum_{i \in N, i \ne j} x_{ij} = 1 \)
3. SubtourElimination (MTZ)
For every pair of cities \( i, j \in N \setminus \{1\}, i \ne j \): \( u_i - u_j + |N| \cdot x_{ij} \le |N| - 1 \). This prevents the formation of subtours that do not include the starting city (city 1).

Type:
Mixed-Integer, linear, linear
MILP
