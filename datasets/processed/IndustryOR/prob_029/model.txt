Set:
1. Feed
The set of available feeds, \( F = \{1, 2, 3, 4, 5\} \)

Parameter:
1. ProteinContent
[3, 2, 1, 6, 18] # Protein (g) per kg of each feed
2. MineralContent
[1, 0.5, 0.2, 2, 0.5] # Minerals (g) per kg of each feed
3. VitaminContent
[0.5, 1, 0.2, 2, 0.8] # Vitamins (mg) per kg of each feed
4. Price
[0.2, 0.7, 0.4, 0.3, 0.8] # Price (Â¥) per kg of each feed
5. MinProtein
700 # Minimum daily protein requirement (g)
6. MinMinerals
30 # Minimum daily mineral requirement (g)
7. MinVitamins
100 # Minimum daily vitamin requirement (mg)

Decision variable:
1. FeedQuantity
Continuous variable, \( x_{f} \) for all \( f \in F \), representing the quantity (in kg) of feed \( f \) to use.

Objective:
Minimize the total cost of the feed mixture.
min: \sum_{f \in F} Price[f] * x_{f}

Constraint:
1. Protein requirement
\sum_{f \in F} ProteinContent[f] * x_{f} \geq MinProtein
2. Mineral requirement
\sum_{f \in F} MineralContent[f] * x_{f} \geq MinMinerals
3. Vitamin requirement
\sum_{f \in F} VitaminContent[f] * x_{f} \geq MinVitamins
4. Non-negativity
\( x_{f} \geq 0, \forall f \in F \)

Type:
Continuous, linear, linear
