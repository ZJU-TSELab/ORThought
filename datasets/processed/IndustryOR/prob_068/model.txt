Set:
1. CuttingPatterns
The set of possible cutting patterns for a 10-meter raw steel bar.
- Pattern A: Three 3-meter pieces (waste 1m)
- Pattern B: Two 3-meter pieces and one 4-meter piece (waste 0m)
- Pattern C: Two 4-meter pieces (waste 2m)

Parameter:
1. Demand
[90, 60] # Required number of 3-meter and 4-meter pieces.
2. PatternYield
[[3, 0], [2, 1], [0, 2]] # Yield of 3-meter and 4-meter pieces for each pattern (A, B, C).

Decision variable:
1. NumBarsCut
Integer variable, \( x_p \) for \( p \in \{\text{A, B, C}\} \), represents the number of raw steel bars cut using pattern p.

Objective:
Minimize: \( x_A + x_B + x_C \)

Constraint:
1. Demand3m
The total number of 3-meter pieces produced must meet the demand: \( 3x_A + 2x_B \ge 90 \)
2. Demand4m
The total number of 4-meter pieces produced must meet the demand: \( x_B + 2x_C \ge 60 \)

Type:
Integer, linear, linear
ILP
