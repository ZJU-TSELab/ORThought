Set:
1. ToyTypes
The set of toy types, \( T = \{\text{High-end, Mid-range, Low-end}\} \).

Parameter:
1. LaborHours
[17, 10, 2] # Manufacturing labor hours required per unit for each toy type.
2. InspectionHours
[8, 4, 2] # Inspection hours required per unit for each toy type.
3. UnitProfit
[300, 200, 100] # Profit per unit for each toy type.
4. AvailableLabor
1000 # Total available manufacturing labor hours.
5. AvailableInspection
500 # Total available inspection hours.
6. MaxDemand
[50, 80, 150] # Maximum market demand for each toy type.

Decision variable:
1. ProductionQuantity
Integer variable, \( x_t \) for \( t \in T \), represents the number of units of toy type t to produce.

Objective:
Maximize: \( 300x_{\text{High-end}} + 200x_{\text{Mid-range}} + 100x_{\text{Low-end}} \)

Constraint:
1. LaborConstraint
The total labor hours used cannot exceed the available hours: \( 17x_{\text{High-end}} + 10x_{\text{Mid-range}} + 2x_{\text{Low-end}} \le 1000 \)
2. InspectionConstraint
The total inspection hours used cannot exceed the available hours: \( 8x_{\text{High-end}} + 4x_{\text{Mid-range}} + 2x_{\text{Low-end}} \le 500 \)
3. DemandConstraint
The production quantity for each toy type cannot exceed its market demand:
- \( x_{\text{High-end}} \le 50 \)
- \( x_{\text{Mid-range}} \le 80 \)
- \( x_{\text{Low-end}} \le 150 \)

Type:
Integer, linear, linear
