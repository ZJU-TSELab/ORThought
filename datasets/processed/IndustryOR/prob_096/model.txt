# TODO: Add model description

Set:
1. RawMaterials
The set of raw materials, \( R = \{A, B, C, D\} \).
2. Products
The set of products, \( P = \{A, B\} \).

Parameter:
1. SulfurContent
[0.03, 0.01, 0.02, 0.01] # Sulfur content for each raw material.
2. PurchasePrice
[6, 16, 10, 15] # Purchase price in thousand yuan/ton for each raw material.
3. SellingPrice
[9.15, 9.15] # Selling price in thousand yuan/ton for each product.
4. MaxSulfur
[0.025, 0.015] # Maximum allowed sulfur content for each product.
5. MaxSupplyD
50 # Maximum supply in tons for raw material D.
6. MaxDemand
[100, 200] # Maximum market demand in tons for each product.

Decision variable:
1. BlendQty
Continuous variable, \( x_{ij} \), the tons of raw material \( i \in R \) used to produce product \( j \in P \).

Objective:
Maximize the total profit. Profit is the total revenue from selling products minus the total cost of purchasing raw materials.
max: \sum_{j \in P} \text{SellingPrice}[j] * (\sum_{i \in R} x_{ij}) - \sum_{i \in R} \sum_{j \in P} \text{PurchasePrice}[i] * x_{ij}

Constraint:
1. Sulfur Content
The total sulfur from the raw materials in a product cannot exceed the maximum allowed percentage of the final product's weight.
\sum_{i \in R} \text{SulfurContent}[i] * x_{ij} \leq \text{MaxSulfur}[j] * (\sum_{i \in R} x_{ij}), \forall j \in P
2. Supply Limit
The total amount of raw material D used cannot exceed its supply limit.
\sum_{j \in P} x_{D,j} \leq \text{MaxSupplyD}
3. Demand Limit
The total amount of each product produced cannot exceed its market demand.
\sum_{i \in R} x_{ij} \leq \text{MaxDemand}[j], \forall j \in P
4. Non-negativity
x_{ij} \geq 0, \forall i \in R, j \in P

Type:
Continuous, linear, linear
LP
