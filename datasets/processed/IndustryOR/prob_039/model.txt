Set:
1. Commodity
The set of commodities, \( C = \{steel, engines, electronics, plastic\} \)

Parameter:
1. UnitPrice
{'steel': 500, 'engines': 1500, 'electronics': 300, 'plastic': 1200}
2. InputRequirements
{'steel': {'engines': 0.02, 'plastic': 0.01}, 'engines': {'steel': 0.8, 'electronics': 0.15, 'plastic': 0.11}, 'electronics': {'steel': 0.01, 'engines': 0.01, 'plastic': 0.05}, 'plastic': {'steel': 0.2, 'engines': 0.03, 'electronics': 0.05}}
3. ImportedGoodsCost
{'steel': 250, 'engines': 300, 'electronics': 50, 'plastic': 300}
4. LaborRequirements
{'steel': 6, 'engines': 12, 'electronics': 6, 'plastic': 24}
5. ProductionLimits
{'engines': 650000, 'plastic': 60000}
6. TotalLabor
830000

Decision variable:
1. ProductionQuantity
Continuous variable, \( x_{c} \) for \( c \in C \), representing the total quantity of commodity \( c \) to produce.
2. NetExport
Continuous variable, \( e_{c} \) for \( c \in C \), representing the net export quantity of commodity \( c \).

Objective:
Maximize the domestic GDP. GDP is defined as the value of net exports minus the cost of other imported goods.
max: \sum_{c \in C} UnitPrice[c] * e_{c} - \sum_{c \in C} ImportedGoodsCost[c] * x_{c}

Constraint:
1. Commodity balance
Net export of a commodity is its production minus the amount consumed domestically by other production processes.
\( e_{c} = x_{c} - \sum_{i \in C} InputRequirements[i,c] * x_{i}, \forall c \in C \)
2. Production limits
Production of certain commodities cannot exceed their limits.
\( x_{engines} \leq ProductionLimits[engines] \)
\( x_{plastic} \leq ProductionLimits[plastic] \)
3. Labor constraint
The total labor used in production cannot exceed the available labor force.
\sum_{c \in C} LaborRequirements[c] * x_{c} \leq TotalLabor
4. Non-negativity
\( x_{c} \geq 0, \forall c \in C \)
\( e_{c} \) can be positive (export) or negative (import).

Type:
Continuous, linear, linear
