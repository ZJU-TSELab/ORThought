Set:
1. Warehouses
The set of warehouses, \( W = \{Verona, Perugia, Rome, Pescara, Taranto, Lamezia\} \)
2. Ports
The set of ports, \( P = \{Genoa, Venice, Ancona, Naples, Bari\} \)

Parameter:
1. Supply
[10, 12, 20, 24, 18, 40] # Empty containers available at each warehouse.
2. Demand
[20, 15, 25, 33, 21] # Container demand at each port.
3. Distances
A 6x5 matrix of distances (km) from each warehouse to each port.
4. CostPerKm
30 # Cost in euros per kilometer.
5. TruckCapacity
2 # Containers per truck.

Decision variable:
1. ContainersShipped
Integer variable, \( x_{w,p} \), number of containers shipped from warehouse \( w \) to port \( p \).
2. TrucksUsed
Integer variable, \( y_{w,p} \), number of trucks used on the route from warehouse \( w \) to port \( p \).

Objective:
Minimize the total transportation cost.
min: \(\sum_{w \in W} \sum_{p \in P} CostPerKm \cdot Distances_{w,p} \cdot x_{w,p}\)

Constraint:
1. Supply Constraint
\(\sum_{p \in P} x_{w,p} \leq Supply_w, \forall w \in W\)
2. Demand Fulfillment
\(\sum_{w \in W} x_{w,p} \geq Demand_p, \forall p \in P\)
3. Truck Capacity
\(x_{w,p} \leq TruckCapacity \cdot y_{w,p}, \forall w \in W, p \in P\)
4. Non-negativity
All decision variables \(\geq 0\).

Type:
Integer, linear, linear
ILP
