Set:
1. Animals
The set of animals to raise, \( A = \{Cows, Sheep, Chickens\} \)

Parameter:
1. SellingPrice
[500, 200, 8] # Selling price for cows, sheep, and chickens.
2. FeedCost
[100, 80, 5] # Feed cost for cows, sheep, and chickens.
3. ManureProduction
[10, 5, 3] # Units of manure produced per day by each cow, sheep, and chicken.
4. MaxManure
800 # Maximum daily manure units the farm can handle.
5. MaxChickens
50 # Maximum number of chickens the farm can hold.
6. MinCows
10 # Minimum number of cows required.
7. MinSheep
20 # Minimum number of sheep required.
8. MaxTotalAnimals
100 # Maximum total number of animals the farm can hold.

Decision variable:
1. NumberOfAnimals
Integer variable, \( x_a \) for each animal type \( a \in A \), representing the number of animals of that type to raise.

Objective:
Maximize the total profit (Total Selling Price - Total Feed Cost).
max: \(\sum_{a \in A} (SellingPrice_a - FeedCost_a) \cdot x_a\)

Constraint:
1. Manure Capacity
\(\sum_{a \in A} ManureProduction_a \cdot x_a \leq MaxManure\)
2. Chicken Capacity
\(x_{Chickens} \leq MaxChickens\)
3. Cow Demand
\(x_{Cows} \geq MinCows\)
4. Sheep Demand
\(x_{Sheep} \geq MinSheep\)
5. Total Animal Capacity
\(\sum_{a \in A} x_a \leq MaxTotalAnimals\)
6. Non-negativity
\(x_a \geq 0, \forall a \in A\)

Type:
Integer, linear, linear
ILP
