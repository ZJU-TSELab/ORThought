Set:
1. Quarter
The set of quarters, \( Q = \{1, 2, ..., QuarterNum\} \)

Parameter:
1. QuarterNum
6
2. VaultCapacity
500
3. InitialInventory
150
4. SellingPrice
[30000, 35000, 40000, 36000, 38000, 42000]
5. PurchasePrice
[32000, 36000, 38000, 33000, 39000, 40000]

Decision variable:
1. SellingAmount
Continuous variable, \( SellingAmount_q \forall q \in Q \), where \( SellingAmount_q \) is the amount of platinum sold in quarter \( q \).
2. PurchaseAmount
Continuous variable, \( PurchaseAmount_q \forall q \in Q \), where \( PurchaseAmount_q \) is the amount of platinum purchased in quarter \( q \).
3. Inventory
Continuous variable, \( Inventory_q \forall q \in Q \), where \( Inventory_q \) is the amount of platinum in the vault at the end of quarter \( q \).

Objective:
1. Maximize the total profit from selling platinum. 
max: \sum_{q \in Q} (SellingPrice[q] * SellingAmount[q] - PurchasePrice[q] * PurchaseAmount[q])

Constraint:
1. Initial inventory constraint.
Inventory[1] = InitialInventory + PurchaseAmount[1] - SellingAmount[1]
2. Inventory balance constraint.
Inventory[q] = Inventory[q-1] + PurchaseAmount[q] - SellingAmount[q] \forall q \in Q, q > 1
3. Inventory capacity constraint.
Inventory[q] \leq VaultCapacity \forall q \in Q
4. Selling amount constraint.
SellingAmount[q] \leq Inventory[q-1] \forall q \in Q, q > 1
5. Initial selling amount constraint.
SellingAmount[1] \leq InitialInventory
6. Non-negativity constraint.
PurchaseAmount[q] \geq 0 \forall q \in Q
SellingAmount[q] \geq 0 \forall q \in Q
7. Inventory non-negativity constraint.
Inventory[q] \geq 0 \forall q \in Q

Type:
Continuous, Linear, Linear
LP
