Set:
1. Bakery
The set of bakeries, \( B = \{1, 2, ..., BakeryNum\} \)
2. Store
The set of stores, \( S = \{1, 2, ..., StoreNum\} \)

Parameter:
1. BakeryNum
4
2. StoreNum
5
3. BakerySupply
[200, 150, 250, 180]
4. StoreDemand
[120, 100, 130, 90, 80]
5. ShippingDistance
[
    [10, 15, 20, 25, 30],
    [12, 8, 18, 22, 28],
    [14, 10, 16, 20, 26],
    [16, 12, 14, 18, 24]
]
6. ShippingCost
[
    [sqrt(10), sqrt(15), sqrt(20), sqrt(25), sqrt(30)],
    [sqrt(12), sqrt(8), sqrt(18), sqrt(22), sqrt(28)],
    [sqrt(14), sqrt(10), sqrt(16), sqrt(20), sqrt(26)],
    [sqrt(16), sqrt(12), sqrt(14), sqrt(18), sqrt(24)]
]

Decision variable:
1. ShipAmount
Integer variable, \( ShipAmount_{b,s} \forall b \in B, s \in S \), where \( ShipAmount_{b,s} \) is the amount of bread shipped from bakery \( b \) to store \( s \).

Objective:
1. Minimize the total transportation cost required to deliver the bread.
min: \sum_{b \in B} \sum_{s \in S} ShippingCost[b][s] * ShipAmount[b][s]

Constraint:
1. Bakery supply constraint.
\sum_{s \in S} ShipAmount[b][s] \leq BakerySupply[b] \forall b \in B
2. Store demand constraint.
\sum_{b \in B} ShipAmount[b][s] = StoreDemand[s] \forall s \in S

Type:
Integer, Linear, Linear
ILP
