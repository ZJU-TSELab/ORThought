Set:
1. Product
The set of products, \( P = \{1, 2\} \)  # 1: packaged fresh apples, 2: bottled apple juice
2. Grade
The set of apple grades the company has, \( G = \{7, 4\} \)

Parameter:
1. Inventory
[90000, 110000]
2. Revenue
[0.35, 1.10]
3. VariableCost
[0.12, 0.80]
4. AverageGrade
[5, 6]
5. MaxProcessingCapacity
150000
6. MinPackagePercentage
0.20

Decision variable:
1. ProcessedAmount
Continuous variable, \( ProcessedAmount[p][g] \forall p \in P, g \in G \), where \( ProcessedAmount[p][g] \) is the amount of apples of grade \( g \) used for product \( p \).

Objective:
1. Maximize the profit from selling apples.
max: \sum_{p \in P} \sum_{g \in G} (Revenue[p] - VariableCost[p]) * ProcessedAmount[p][g]

Constraint:
1. Inventory constraint.
\sum_{p \in P} ProcessedAmount[p][g] \leq Inventory[g] \forall g \in G
2. Average grade constraint.
AverageGrade[p] * \sum_{g \in G} ProcessedAmount[p][g] \leq \sum_{g \in G} g * ProcessedAmount[p][g] \forall p \in P
3. Processing capacity constraint.
\sum_{p \in P} \sum_{g \in G} ProcessedAmount[p][g] \leq MaxProcessingCapacity
4. Marketing constraint.
\sum_{g \in G} ProcessedAmount[1][g] \geq MinPackagePercentage * \sum_{p \in P} \sum_{g \in G} ProcessedAmount[p][g]
5. Non-negativity constraint.
ProcessedAmount[p][g] \geq 0 \forall p \in P, g \in G

Type:
Continuous, Linear, Linear
