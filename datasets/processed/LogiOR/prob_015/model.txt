Decision variable:
1. X
Binary variable, \( x_{gks} \forall g \in G, k \in K, s \in S\), where \( x_{gks} = 1 \) if good \( g \) is loaded on truck \( k \) on side \( s \), otherwise \( x_{gks} = 0 \).
2. Y
Binary variable, \( y_{k} \forall k \in K\), where \( y_{k} = 1 \) if truck \( k \) is used, otherwise \( y_{k} = 0 \).

Objective:
The minimum number of trucks used.
min: \sum_{k \in K} y_{k}

Constraint:
1. Each good must be loaded on one truck side.
\sum_{k \in K} \sum_{s \in S} x_{gks} = 1 \forall g \in G
2. Truck capacity side constraint.
\sum_{g \in G} GoodsWeight[g] * x_{gks} <= TruckMaxCapacitySide[s] \forall k \in K, s \in S
3. Truck side difference constraint.
0 <= \sum_{g \in G} GoodsWeight[g] * x_{gk0} - \sum_{g \in G} GoodsWeight[g] * x_{gk1} <= TruckMaxWeightDifference \forall k \in K
4. Truck thickness constraint.
\sum_{g \in G} GoodsThickness[g] * x_{gks} <= TruckMaxThickness \forall k \in K, s \in S
5. Truck used constraint 1.
\sum_{g \in G}\sum_{s \in S} x_{gks} >= y_{k} \forall k \in K
6. Truck used constraint 2.
y_{k} >= x_{gks} \forall g \in G, k \in K, s \in S
