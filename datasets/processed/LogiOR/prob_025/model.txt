Set:
1. Month
The set of months, \( M = \{1, 2, ..., MonthNum\} \)

Parameter:
1. MonthNum
6
2. RegularTimeProductionLimit
6000
3. OvertimeProductionLimit
2000
4. RegularTimeCost
2
5. OvertimeCost
3
6. InventoryHoldingCost
0.5
7. Demand
[5000, 6000, 5500, 7000, 4500, 4000]

Decision variable:
1. RegularTimeProduction
Integer variable, \( RegularTimeProduction_m \forall m \in M \), where \( RegularTimeProduction_m \) is the amount of books produced in month \( m \) during regular time.
2. OvertimeProduction
Integer variable, \( OvertimeProduction_m \forall m \in M \), where \( OvertimeProduction_m \) is the amount of books produced in month \( m \) during overtime.
3. Inventory
Integer variable, \( Inventory_m \forall m \in M \), where \( Inventory_m \) is the amount of books in inventory at the end of month \( m \).

Objective:
1. Minimize the total cost of production and inventory holding.
min: \sum_{m \in M} (RegularTimeCost * RegularTimeProduction[m] + OvertimeCost * OvertimeProduction[m] + InventoryHoldingCost * Inventory[m])

Constraint:
1. Initial inventory constraint.
Inventory[1] = 0 + RegularTimeProduction[1] + OvertimeProduction[1] - Demand[1]
2. Inventory balance constraint.
Inventory[m] = Inventory[m-1] + RegularTimeProduction[m] + OvertimeProduction[m] - Demand[m] \forall m \in M, m > 1
3. Demand constraint.
RegularTimeProduction[1] + OvertimeProduction[1] \geq Demand[1] 
Inventory[m-1]+ RegularTimeProduction[m] + OvertimeProduction[m] \geq Demand[m] \forall m \in M, m > 1
4. Regular time production limit constraint.
RegularTimeProduction[m] \leq RegularTimeProductionLimit \forall m \in M
5. Overtime production limit constraint.
OvertimeProduction[m] \leq OvertimeProductionLimit \forall m \in M
6. Non-negativity constraint.
RegularTimeProduction[m] \geq 0 \forall m \in M
OvertimeProduction[m] \geq 0 \forall m \in M
Inventory[m] \geq 0 \forall m \in M

Type:
Integer, Linear, Linear
