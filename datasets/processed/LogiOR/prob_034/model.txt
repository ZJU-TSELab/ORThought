Set:
1. Fuel
The set of fuels, \( F = \{1, 2\} \)  # 1: gasoline, 2: diesel
2. Feedstock
The set of feedstocks, \( S = \{1, 2\} \)  # 1: feedstock A, 2: feedstock B

Parameter:
1. Inventory
[8000, 12000]
2. QualityRating
[12, 6]
3. Revenue
[30, 25]
4. DemandperMarketingDollar
[8, 15]
5. MinQualityRating
[10, 8]

Decision variable:
1. ProductionAmount
Continuous variable, \( ProductionAmount[f][s] \forall f \in F, s \in S \), where \( ProductionAmount[f][s] \) is the amount of fuel \( f \) produced from feedstock \( s \).
2. MarketingBudget
Continuous variable, \( MarketingBudget[f] \forall f \in F \), where \( MarketingBudget[f] \) is the marketing budget for fuel \( f \).

Objective:
1. Maximize the profit from selling fuels.
max: \sum_{f \in F} Revenue[f] * \sum_{s \in S} ProductionAmount[f][s] - \sum_{f \in F} MarketingBudget[f]

Constraint:
1. Inventory constraint.
\sum_{f \in F} ProductionAmount[f][s] \leq Inventory[s] \forall s \in S
2. Quality rating constraint.
\sum_{s \in S} QualityRating[s] * ProductionAmount[f][s] \geq MinQualityRating[f] * \sum_{s \in S} ProductionAmount[f][s] \forall f \in F
3. Demand constraint.
DemandperMarketingDollar[f] * MarketingBudget[f] \geq \sum_{s \in S} ProductionAmount[f][s] \forall f \in F
4. Non-negativity constraint.
ProductionAmount[f][s] \geq 0 \forall f \in F, s \in S
MarketingBudget[f] \geq 0 \forall f \in F

Type:
Continuous, Linear, Linear
LP
