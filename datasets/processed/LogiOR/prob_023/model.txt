Set:
1. Chemical
The set of chemicals, \( C = \{1, 2, 3, 4\} \)
2. Ingredient
The set of ingredients, \( I = \{1, 2, 3\} \)

Parameter:
1. ChemicalID
['1', '2', '3', '4']
2. IngredientID
['A', 'B', 'C']
3. ChemicalCost
[8, 10, 11, 14]
4. IngredientContent
# units/lb
[
[0.03, 0.02, 0.01],
[0.06, 0.04, 0.01],
[0.10, 0.03, 0.04],
[0.12, 0.09, 0.04]
]
5. IngredientMin
[0.08, 0.04, 0.02]
6. ChemicalMin
[0, 100, 0, 0]
7. ProduceTarget
1000

Decision variable:
1. ChemicalAmount
Continuous variable, \( ChemicalAmount_i \forall i \in C \), where \( ChemicalAmount_i \) is the amount of chemical \( i \) used in the production of Panacea.

Objective:
1. Minimize the total cost of chemicals used.
min: \sum_{i \in C} ChemicalCost[i] * ChemicalAmount[i]

Constraint:
1. Ingredient minimum content. 
\sum_{i \in C} IngredientContent[i][j] * ChemicalAmount[i] \geq IngredientMin[j] * ProduceTarget \forall j \in I
2. Chemical minimum content.
ChemicalAmount[i] \geq ChemicalMin[i] \forall i \in C
3. Total amount of chemicals used.
\sum_{i \in C} ChemicalAmount[i] = ProduceTarget
4. Non-negativity constraint.
ChemicalAmount[i] \geq 0 \forall i \in C

Type:
Continuous, Linear, Linear
LP
