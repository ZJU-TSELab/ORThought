Set:
1. Mines 
The set of mines, \( M = \{M1, M2, M3, M4\} \)
2. Plants
The set of processing plants, \( P = \{P1, P2, P3\} \)

Parameter:
1. MineSupply  
\( s_m \): Available ore at mine m (tons)  
[M1:6000, M2:9000, M3:7000, M4:5000]  
2. PlantCapacity  
\( c_p \): Processing capacity of plant p (tons)  
[P1:8000, P2:10000, P3:12000]  
3. RecoveryRate  
\( r_m \): Metal recovery rate for mine m (%)  
[M1:0.85, M2:0.92, M3:0.78, M4:0.88]  
4. ProcessingCost  
\( pc_m \): Processing cost per ton for mine m ($)  
[M1:12, M2:18, M3:10, M4:15]  
5. SulfurContent  
\( sc_m \): Sulfur content for mine m (%)  
[M1:1.2, M2:0.8, M3:2.1, M4:1.5]  
6. TransportCost  
\( tc_{mp} \): Transportation cost from mine m to plant p ($)  
(As given in the cost matrix)  
7. MaxSulfur  
1.5 (%) Maximum allowed average sulfur content  
8. Budget  
500000 ($) Total cost limit  

Decision variable:
1. Allocation  
\( x_{mp} \geq 0 \): Continuous variable representing tons of ore shipped from mine m to plant p  

Objective:  
Maximize total metal recovery  
max: \( \sum_{m\in M} \sum_{p\in P} r_m \cdot x_{mp} \)

Constraint:
1. Mine supply limit  
\( \sum_{p\in P} x_{mp} \leq s_m, \forall m \in M \)
2. Plant capacity limit  
\( \sum_{m\in M} x_{mp} \leq c_p, \forall p \in P \)
3. Total cost limit (processing + transportation)  
\( \sum_{m\in M} \sum_{p\in P} (pc_m + tc_{mp}) \cdot x_{mp} \leq 500000 \)
4. Average sulfur content restriction  
\( \frac{\sum_{m\in M} \sum_{p\in P} sc_m \cdot x_{mp}}{\sum_{m\in M} \sum_{p\in P} x_{mp}} \leq 1.5 \)
5. Non-negativity  
\( x_{mp} \geq 0, \forall m \in M, p \in P \)

Type:
Continuous, Linear, Linear  
LP
