Set:
1. DistributionCenter
The set of distribution centers, \( D = \{1, 2, ..., DistributionCenterNum\} \)
2. FulfillmentHub
The set of fulfillment hubs, \( H = \{1, 2, ..., FulfillmentHubNum\} \)
3. ProductType
The set of product types, \( P = \{1, 2, ..., ProductTypeNum\} \) 

Parameter:
1. DistributionCenterNum
3
2. FulfillmentHubNum
3
3. ProductTypeNum
2
4. HubInventoryRequirement
300
5. StoreStandardProducts
[150, 250, 300]
6. StorePremiumProducts
[30, 100, 70]
7. ShippingDistance
[
    [0, 12, 18],
    [12, 0, 15],
    [18, 15, 0]
]

Decision variable:
1. ShipAmount
Integer variable, \( ShipAmount_{d,h,p} \forall d \in D, h \in H, p \in P \), where \( ShipAmount_{d,h,p} \) is the amount of product type \( p \) shipped from distribution center \( d \) to fulfillment hub \( h \).

Objective:
1. Minimize the total transportation distance required to move the products.
min: \sum_{d \in D} \sum_{h \in H} \sum_{p \in P} (ShippingDistance[d][h] * ShipAmount[d][h][p])

Constraint:
1. Fulfillment hub inventory constraint.
HubInventoryRequirement = \sum_{d \in D} \sum_{p \in P} ShipAmount[d][h][p] \forall h \in H
2. Product type balance constraint.
ShipAmount[d][h][1] \leq StoreStandardProducts[d] \forall d \in D, h \in H


Type:
Integer, Linear, Linear
ILP
