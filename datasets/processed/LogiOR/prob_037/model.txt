Set:
1. Product
The set of products, \( P = \{1, 2, 3, 4\} \)  # 1: Basic Product, 2: Advanced Product, 3: Premium Basic, 4: Premium Advanced

Parameter:
1. ProductPerRawMaterial
[2, 3, 2, 3]
2. LaborHoursPerRawMaterial
2
3. LaborHoursPerProduct
[0, 0, 3, 4]
4. SellingPrice
[10, 12, 20, 25] 
5. ProcessingCost
[0, 0, 5, 6]
6. RawMaterialCost
5
7. MaxLaborHours
8000
8. MaxRawMaterial
3000

Decision variable:
1. RawMaterialPurchased
Integer variable, \( RawMaterialPurchased \), the amount of raw material purchased in units.
2. ProductProduced
Integer variable, \( ProductProduced[p] \forall p \in P \), the amount of product \( p \) produced in units.

Objective:
1. Maximize the total profit.
max: \sum_{p \in P} SellingPrice[p] * ProductProduced[p] - RawMaterialCost * RawMaterialPurchased - \sum_{p \in P} ProcessingCost[p] * ProductProduced[p]

Constraint:
1. Labor hours constraint.
LaborHoursPerRawMaterial * RawMaterialPurchased + \sum_{p \in P} LaborHoursPerProduct[p] * ProductProduced[p] \leq MaxLaborHours
2. Raw material constraint.
RawMaterialPurchased \leq MaxRawMaterial
3. Product production constraint.
\sum_{p \in P}ProductProduced[p] == \sum_{p \in P}ProductPerRawMaterial[p] * RawMaterialPurchased
4. Non-negativity constraint.
ProductProduced[p] \geq 0 \forall p \in P
RawMaterialPurchased \geq 0

Type:
Integer, Linear, Linear
ILP
