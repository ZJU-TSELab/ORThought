Set:
1. Components
The set of components, \( C = \{1, 2, 3, 4, 5\} \) representing C1-C5
2. Suppliers
The set of suppliers, \( S = \{1, 2, 3\} \) representing S1-S3

Parameter:
1. Demand
[1200, 800, 1500, 900, 600] units for each \( c \in C \)
2. Price
\( p_{scsc} \): Price from supplier \( s \) for component \( c \),
S1: [12,15,18,20,22], S2: [14,16,17,19,21], S3: [13,14,19,18,20]
3. Reliability
\( r_s \): [0.85, 0.75, 0.90] for S1-S3
4. Capacity
\( cap_s \): [2000, 1800, 2500] units for S1-S3
5. MinReliableFraction
0
.6 (minimum fraction from reliable suppliers)
6. MaxSupplierSpendFraction
0.4 (maximum spend share per supplier)

Decision variable:
1. ProcurementQuantity
\( q_{sc} \geq 0 \) for all \( s \in S, c \in C \), quantity purchased from supplier \( s \) for component \( c \)

Objective:
Minimize total procurement cost
min: \( \sum_{s\in S}\sum_{c\in C} p_{sc} \cdot q_{sc} \)

Constraint:
1. Demand satisfaction
\( \sum_{s\in S} q_{sc} = Demand_c, \forall c \in C \)
2. Supplier capacity
\( \sum_{c\in C} q_{sc} \leq cap_s, \forall s \in S \)
3. Reliable sourcing requirement
\( \sum_{s\in S | r_s \geq 0.8} q_{sc} \geq 0.6 \cdot Demand_c, \forall c \in C \)
4. Spend concentration limit
\( \sum_{c\in C} p_{sc} \cdot q_{sc} \leq 0.4 \cdot \sum_{s'\in S}\sum_{c\in C} p_{s'c} \cdot q_{s'c}, \forall s \in S \)

Type:
Continuous, Linear, Linear
