Set:
1. Month
The set of months, \( M = \{1, 2, \ldots, 12\} \)  # 1: January, 2: February, ..., 12: December

Parameter:
1. OperationalHoursNeeded
[600, 450, 350, 800, 1000, 450, 350, 600, 450, 350, 250, 150]  # operational hours needed for each month
2. NormalHoursPerSupervisorPerMonth
160  # hours each supervisor can handle per month
3. OvertimeHoursPerSupervisorPerMonth
40  # additional hours each supervisor can work per month as overtime
4. SupervisorsVacationMonths
1
5. FixedSalaryPerSupervisorPerMonth
5000  # fixed monthly salary for each supervisor
6. OvertimeRatePerHour
45  # overtime pay rate per hour

Decision variable:
1. SupervisorsNeeded
Integer variable, \( SupervisorsNeeded \), the number of warehouse supervisors needed.
2. WorkSupervisorPerMonth
Integer variable, \( WorkSupervisorPerMonth[m] \forall m \in M \), the number of hours worked by supervisors in month \( m \).
3. OvertimeHoursPerMonth
Integer variable, \( OvertimeHoursPerMonth[m] \forall m \in M \), the total overtime hours worked by supervisors in month \( m \).

Objective:
1. Minimize the total cost.
min: FixedSalaryPerSupervisorPerMonth \times SupervisorsNeeded \times 12 + \sum_{m \in M} (OvertimeRatePerHour \times OvertimeHoursPerMonth[m])

Constraint:
1. Operational hours constraint.
WorkSupervisorPerMonth[m] \times NormalHoursPerSupervisorPerMonth + OvertimeHoursPerMonth[m] \geq OperationalHoursNeeded[m] \forall m \in M
2. Vacation constraint.
\sum_{m \in M} WorkSupervisorPerMonth[m] = (12 - SupervisorsVacationMonths) \times SupervisorsNeeded
3. Supervisors number constraint.
WorkSupervisorPerMonth[m] \leq SupervisorsNeeded \forall m \in M
4. Overtime hours constraint.
OvertimeHoursPerMonth[m] \leq WorkSupervisorPerMonth[m] \times OvertimeHoursPerSupervisorPerMonth \forall m \in M
5. Non-negativity constraint.
SupervisorsNeeded \geq 0
WorkSupervisorPerMonth[m] \geq 0 \forall m \in M

Type:
Integer, Linear, Linear
ILP
