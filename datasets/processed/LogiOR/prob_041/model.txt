Set:
1. Brewery
The set of breweries, \( B = \{1, 2\} \)  # 1: Portland, 2: Asheville
2. DestinationHub
The set of destination hubs, \( H = \{1, 2\} \)  # 1: Seattle, 2: Atlanta
3. Year
The set of years, \( Y = \{1, 2, 3\} \)

Parameter:
1. ProductionCapacity
[200000, 300000]  # in gallons
2. ShippingCapacity
[400000, 400000]  # in gallons
3. ProfitPerThousandGallons
[[500, 450], [480, 520]]  # in dollars per thousand gallons
4. ExpansionCost
[1000, 1500]  # in dollars per thousand gallons

Decision variable:
1. ProductionCapacityAdded
Continous variable, \( ProductionCapacityAdded[b] \forall b \in B \), the amount of production capacity added for brewery \( b \) (in thousands of gallons).
2. ProductionFromBrewery2Hub
Continous variable, \( ProductionFromBrewery2Hub[b][h][y] \forall b \in B, h \in H, y \in Y \), the amount of product shipped from brewery \( b \) to hub \( h \) in year \( y \) (in thousands of gallons).

Objective:
1. Maximize the total profit.
max: \sum_{b \in B, h \in H, y \in Y} (ProductionFromBrewery2Hub[b][h][y] * ProfitPerThousandGallons[b][h]) - \sum_{b \in B} (ProductionCapacityAdded[b] * ExpansionCost[b])

Constraint:
1. Production capacity constraint.
\sum_{h \in H} ProductionFromBrewery2Hub[b][h][y] \leq (ProductionCapacity[b] + ProductionCapacityAdded[b]) \forall b \in B, y \in Y
2. Shipping capacity constraint.
\sum_{b \in B} ProductionFromBrewery2Hub[b][h][y] \leq ShippingCapacity[h] \forall h \in H, y \in Y
3. Non-negativity constraint.
ProductionCapacityAdded[b] \geq 0 \forall b \in B
ProductionFromBrewery2Hub[b][h][y] \geq 0 \forall b \in B, h \in H, y \in Y

Type:
Continuous, Linear, Linear
