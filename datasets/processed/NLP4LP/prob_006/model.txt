Set:
1. FurnitureType
The set of furniture types, \( T = \{0, 1\} \) # 0 for Chair, 1 for Dresser

Parameter:
1. Profit
[43, 52] # Profit per furniture type: $43 for Chair, $52 for Dresser
2. StainAvailable
17 # Total gallons of stain available
3. OakAvailable
11 # Total lengths of oak wood available
4. StainRequired
[1.4, 1.1] # Gallons of stain required per furniture type
5. OakRequired
[2, 3] # Lengths of oak wood required per furniture type

Decision variable:
1. FurnitureCount
Integer variable, \( x_{t} \) for all \( t \in T \), representing the number of items of type \( t \) to produce.

Objective:
Maximize the total profit from producing furniture.
max: \sum_{t \in T} Profit[t] * x_{t}

Constraint:
1. Stain constraint
\sum_{t \in T} StainRequired[t] * x_{t} \leq StainAvailable
2. Oak constraint
\sum_{t \in T} OakRequired[t] * x_{t} \leq OakAvailable
3. Non-negativity
x_{t} \geq 0, \forall t \in T

Type:
Integer, linear, linear
