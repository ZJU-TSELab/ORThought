Set:
1. CandyType
The set of candy types, \( I = \{0, 1\} \) # 0 for Peach, 1 for Cherry

Parameter:
1. SyrupUsage
[5, 4] # Units of special syrup required for each candy type
2. PeachFlavoringUsage
3 # Units of peach flavoring required for a pack of peach candy
3. CherryFlavoringUsage
5 # Units of cherry flavoring required for a pack of cherry candy
4. MaxPeachFlavoring
3000 # Units of peach flavoring available
5. MaxCherryFlavoring
4000 # Units of cherry flavoring available
6. MinCherryPercentage
0.3 # Minimum percentage of cherry flavored candy packs

Decision variable:
1. CandyPackCount
Integer variable, \( x_{i} \) for all \( i \in I \), representing the number of packs of candy of type \( i \) to be made.

Objective:
Minimize the total amount of special syrup used.
min: \( \sum_{i \in I} SyrupUsage[i] \cdot x_{i} \)

Constraint:
1. Peach flavoring constraint
\( PeachFlavoringUsage \cdot x_{0} \leq MaxPeachFlavoring \)
2. Cherry flavoring constraint
\( CherryFlavoringUsage \cdot x_{1} \leq MaxCherryFlavoring \)
3. Popularity constraint
\( x_{0} \geq x_{1} + 1 \)
4. Cherry percentage constraint
\( x_{1} \geq MinCherryPercentage \cdot (x_{0} + x_{1}) \) which simplifies to \( 7 \cdot x_{1} \geq 3 \cdot x_{0} \)
5. Non-negativity
\( x_{i} \geq 0 \), for all \( i \in I \)

Type:
Integer, linear, linear
