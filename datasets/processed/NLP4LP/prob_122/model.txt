Set:
1. PillType
The set of pill types, \( I = \{0, 1\} \) # 0 for daytime, 1 for nighttime

Parameter:
1. PainkillerMedicine
[6, 5] # Units of painkiller medicine per pill for each pill type
2. SleepMedicine
[2, 5] # Units of sleep medicine per pill for each pill type
3. MaxPainkillerMedicine
800 # Maximum units of painkiller medicine available
4. MinNighttimePills
200 # Minimum number of nighttime pills to be made
5. MinDaytimePillPercentage
0.4 # Minimum percentage of daytime pills

Decision variable:
1. PillCount
Integer variable, \( x_{i} \) for all \( i \in I \), representing the number of pills of each type to be made.

Objective:
Minimize the total amount of sleep medicine required.
min: \( \sum_{i \in I} SleepMedicine[i] \cdot x_{i} \)

Constraint:
1. Painkiller medicine constraint
\( \sum_{i \in I} PainkillerMedicine[i] \cdot x_{i} \leq MaxPainkillerMedicine \)
2. Daytime pill percentage constraint
\( x_{0} \geq MinDaytimePillPercentage \cdot (x_{0} + x_{1}) \) which simplifies to \( (1 - MinDaytimePillPercentage) \cdot x_{0} \geq MinDaytimePillPercentage \cdot x_{1} \) or \( 0.6 \cdot x_{0} \geq 0.4 \cdot x_{1} \) or \( 3 \cdot x_{0} \geq 2 \cdot x_{1} \)
3. Minimum nighttime pills constraint
\( x_{1} \geq MinNighttimePills \)
4. Non-negativity
\( x_{i} \geq 0 \), for all \( i \in I \)

Type:
Integer, linear, linear
