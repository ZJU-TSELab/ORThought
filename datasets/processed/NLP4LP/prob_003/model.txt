Set:
1. CableType
The set of cable types, \( T = \{0, 1\} \) # 0 for Long, 1 for Short

Parameter:
1. GoldAvailable
1000 # Total gold available in mg
2. GoldRequired
[10, 7] # Gold required per cable type: 10mg for Long, 7mg for Short
3. Profit
[12, 5] # Profit per cable type: $12 for Long, $5 for Short
4. MinLongCables
10 # Minimum number of long cables to be made
5. ShortToLongRatio
5 # The ratio of short cables to long cables must be at least 5

Decision variable:
1. CableCount
Integer variable, \( x_{t} \) for all \( t \in T \), representing the number of cables of type \( t \) to produce.

Objective:
Maximize the total profit from producing cables.
max: \sum_{t \in T} Profit[t] * x_{t}

Constraint:
1. Gold constraint
\sum_{t \in T} GoldRequired[t] * x_{t} \leq GoldAvailable
2. Ratio constraint
x_{1} \geq ShortToLongRatio * x_{0}
3. Long cable minimum
x_{0} \geq MinLongCables
4. Non-negativity
x_{t} \geq 0, \forall t \in T

Type:
Integer, linear, linear
