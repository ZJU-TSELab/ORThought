Set:
1. BagType
The set of bag types, \( I = \{0, 1\} \) # 0 for Large, 1 for Tiny

Parameter:
1. GrainPerBag
[25, 6] # Kilograms of grain each bag type can hold
2. EnergyPerBag
[4, 1.5] # Units of energy required to transport each bag type
3. MaxEnergy
110 # Maximum units of energy available
4. LargeToTinyRatio
2 # The number of large bags must be this many times the number of tiny bags
5. MinTinyBags
20 # Minimum number of tiny bags to be used

Decision variable:
1. NumberOfBags
Integer variable, \( x_{i} \) for all \( i \in I \), representing the number of bags for each type.

Objective:
Maximize the total amount of grain in weight.
max: \( \sum_{i \in I} GrainPerBag[i] \cdot x_{i} \)

Constraint:
1. Energy limit
\( \sum_{i \in I} EnergyPerBag[i] \cdot x_{i} \leq MaxEnergy \)
2. Bag ratio
\( x_{0} = LargeToTinyRatio \cdot x_{1} \)
3. Minimum tiny bags
\( x_{1} \geq MinTinyBags \)
4. Non-negativity
\( x_{i} \geq 0 \), for all \( i \in I \)

Type:
Integer, linear, linear
