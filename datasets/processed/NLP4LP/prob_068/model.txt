Set:
1. CrepeType
The set of crepe types, \( I = \{0, 1\} \) # 0 for Chocolate, 1 for Peanut Butter

Parameter:
1. CrepeMixUsage
[6, 7] # Units of crepe mix required for each crepe type
2. ChocolateSpreadUsage
3 # Units of chocolate spread required for a chocolate crepe
3. PeanutButterSpreadUsage
4 # Units of peanut butter spread required for a peanut butter crepe
4. MaxChocolateSpread
400 # Units of chocolate spread available
5. MaxPeanutButterSpread
450 # Units of peanut butter spread available
6. MinChocolatePercentage
0.25 # Minimum percentage of chocolate crepes

Decision variable:
1. CrepeCount
Integer variable, \( x_{i} \) for all \( i \in I \), representing the number of crepes of type \( i \) to be made.

Objective:
Minimize the total amount of crepe mix needed.
min: \( \sum_{i \in I} CrepeMixUsage[i] \cdot x_{i} \)

Constraint:
1. Chocolate spread constraint
\( ChocolateSpreadUsage \cdot x_{0} \leq MaxChocolateSpread \)
2. Peanut butter spread constraint
\( PeanutButterSpreadUsage \cdot x_{1} \leq MaxPeanutButterSpread \)
3. Popularity constraint
\( x_{1} \geq x_{0} + 1 \)
4. Chocolate percentage constraint
\( x_{0} \geq MinChocolatePercentage \cdot (x_{0} + x_{1}) \) which simplifies to \( 3 \cdot x_{0} \geq x_{1} \)
5. Non-negativity
\( x_{i} \geq 0 \), for all \( i \in I \)

Type:
Integer, linear, linear
