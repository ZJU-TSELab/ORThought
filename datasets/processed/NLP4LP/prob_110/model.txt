Set:
1. FoodType
The set of food types, \( I = \{0, 1\} \) # 0 for ramen, 1 for fries

Parameter:
1. Calories
[400, 300] # Calories per pack for each food type
2. Protein
[20, 10] # Grams of protein per pack for each food type
3. Sodium
[100, 75] # mg of sodium per pack for each food type
4. MinCalories
3000 # Minimum calories required
5. MinProtein
80 # Minimum grams of protein required
6. MaxRamenPercentage
0.3 # Maximum percentage of ramen packs

Decision variable:
1. FoodCount
Continuous variable, \( x_{i} \) for all \( i \in I \), representing the number of packs of each food type.

Objective:
Minimize the total sodium intake.
min: \( \sum_{i \in I} Sodium[i] \cdot x_{i} \)

Constraint:
1. Calorie requirement
\( \sum_{i \in I} Calories[i] \cdot x_{i} \geq MinCalories \)
2. Protein requirement
\( \sum_{i \in I} Protein[i] \cdot x_{i} \geq MinProtein \)
3. Ramen percentage constraint
\( x_{0} \leq MaxRamenPercentage \cdot (x_{0} + x_{1}) \) which simplifies to \( (1 - MaxRamenPercentage) \cdot x_{0} \leq MaxRamenPercentage \cdot x_{1} \) or \( 0.7 \cdot x_{0} \leq 0.3 \cdot x_{1} \) or \( 7 \cdot x_{0} \leq 3 \cdot x_{1} \)
4. Non-negativity
\( x_{i} \geq 0 \), for all \( i \in I \)

Type:
Continuous, linear, linear
