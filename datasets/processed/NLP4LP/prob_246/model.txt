Set:
1. CatalystType
The set of catalyst types, \( I = \{0, 1\} \) # 0 for palladium-heavy, 1 for platinum-heavy

Parameter:
1. ConversionRate
[5, 4] # CO2 conversion rate for each catalyst type (units per second)
2. PlatinumRequired
[15, 20] # Units of platinum required for each catalyst type
3. PalladiumRequired
[25, 14] # Units of palladium required for each catalyst type
4. PlatinumAvailable
450 # Total units of platinum available
5. PalladiumAvailable
390 # Total units of palladium available

Decision variable:
1. NumberOfCatalysts
Continuous variable, \( x_{i} \) for all \( i \in I \), representing the number of catalysts of each type to use.

Objective:
Maximize the total amount converted into carbon dioxide.
max: \( \sum_{i \in I} ConversionRate[i] \cdot x_{i} \)

Constraint:
1. Platinum availability
\( \sum_{i \in I} PlatinumRequired[i] \cdot x_{i} \leq PlatinumAvailable \)
2. Palladium availability
\( \sum_{i \in I} PalladiumRequired[i] \cdot x_{i} \leq PalladiumAvailable \)
3. Non-negativity
\( x_{i} \geq 0 \), for all \( i \in I \)

Type:
Continuous, linear, linear
