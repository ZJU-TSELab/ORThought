Set:
1. FruitType
The set of fruit types, \( I = \{0, 1\} \) # 0 for bananas, 1 for mangoes

Parameter:
1. Calories
[80, 100] # Calories per fruit for each fruit type
2. Potassium
[20, 15] # Grams of potassium per fruit for each fruit type
3. Sugar
[10, 8] # Grams of sugar per fruit for each fruit type
4. MinCalories
4000 # Minimum calories required
5. MinPotassium
150 # Minimum grams of potassium required
6. MaxMangoPercentage
0.33 # Maximum percentage of mangoes

Decision variable:
1. FruitCount
Integer variable, \( x_{i} \) for all \( i \in I \), representing the number of each fruit to be consumed.

Objective:
Minimize the total sugar intake.
min: \( \sum_{i \in I} Sugar[i] \cdot x_{i} \)

Constraint:
1. Calorie requirement
\( \sum_{i \in I} Calories[i] \cdot x_{i} \geq MinCalories \)
2. Potassium requirement
\( \sum_{i \in I} Potassium[i] \cdot x_{i} \geq MinPotassium \)
3. Mango percentage constraint
\( x_{1} \leq MaxMangoPercentage \cdot (x_{0} + x_{1}) \) which simplifies to \( (1 - MaxMangoPercentage) \cdot x_{1} \leq MaxMangoPercentage \cdot x_{0} \) or \( 0.67 \cdot x_{1} \leq 0.33 \cdot x_{0} \) which is approximately \( 2 \cdot x_{1} \leq x_{0} \)
4. Non-negativity
\( x_{i} \geq 0 \), for all \( i \in I \)

Type:
Integer, linear, linear
