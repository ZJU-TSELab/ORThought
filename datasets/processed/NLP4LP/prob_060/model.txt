Set:
1. MachineType
The set of washing machine types, \( I = \{0, 1\} \) # 0 for Top-loading, 1 for Front-loading

Parameter:
1. WashingCapacity
[50, 75] # Items washed per day by each machine type
2. EnergyConsumption
[85, 100] # kWh consumed per day by each machine type
3. MinWashingCapacity
5000 # Minimum total items to be washed per day
4. MaxEnergyConsumption
7000 # Maximum total kWh available per day
5. MaxTopLoadingPercentage
0.4 # Maximum percentage of top-loading machines
6. MinFrontLoadingMachines
10 # Minimum number of front-loading machines

Decision variable:
1. MachineCount
Integer variable, \( x_{i} \) for all \( i \in I \), representing the number of machines of type \( i \) to be bought.

Objective:
Minimize the total number of washing machines.
min: \( \sum_{i \in I} x_{i} \)

Constraint:
1. Washing capacity constraint
\( \sum_{i \in I} WashingCapacity[i] \cdot x_{i} \geq MinWashingCapacity \)
2. Energy consumption constraint
\( \sum_{i \in I} EnergyConsumption[i] \cdot x_{i} \leq MaxEnergyConsumption \)
3. Top-loading proportion constraint
\( x_{0} \leq MaxTopLoadingPercentage \cdot (x_{0} + x_{1}) \) which simplifies to \( 3 \cdot x_{0} \leq 2 \cdot x_{1} \)
4. Minimum front-loading machines constraint
\( x_{1} \geq MinFrontLoadingMachines \)
5. Non-negativity
\( x_{i} \geq 0 \), for all \( i \in I \)

Type:
Integer, linear, linear
