Set:
1. PillType
The set of pill types, \( I = \{0, 1\} \) # 0 for painkiller, 1 for sleeping pill

Parameter:
1. Morphine
[10, 6] # mg of morphine per pill for each pill type
2. DigestiveMedicine
[3, 5] # Units of digestive medicine per pill for each pill type
3. MaxMorphine
3000 # Maximum mg of morphine available
4. MinPainkillerPills
50 # Minimum number of painkiller pills to be made
5. MinSleepingPillPercentage
0.7 # Minimum percentage of sleeping pills

Decision variable:
1. PillCount
Integer variable, \( x_{i} \) for all \( i \in I \), representing the number of pills of each type to be made.

Objective:
Minimize the total amount of digestive medicine needed.
min: \( \sum_{i \in I} DigestiveMedicine[i] \cdot x_{i} \)

Constraint:
1. Morphine constraint
\( \sum_{i \in I} Morphine[i] \cdot x_{i} \leq MaxMorphine \)
2. Sleeping pill percentage constraint
\( x_{1} \geq MinSleepingPillPercentage \cdot (x_{0} + x_{1}) \) which simplifies to \( (1 - MinSleepingPillPercentage) \cdot x_{1} \geq MinSleepingPillPercentage \cdot x_{0} \) or \( 0.3 \cdot x_{1} \geq 0.7 \cdot x_{0} \) or \( 3 \cdot x_{1} \geq 7 \cdot x_{0} \)
3. Minimum painkiller pills constraint
\( x_{0} \geq MinPainkillerPills \)
4. Non-negativity
\( x_{i} \geq 0 \), for all \( i \in I \)

Type:
Integer, linear, linear
