Set:
1. BakeryItem
The set of bakery items, \( I = \{0, 1\} \) # 0 for Brownies, 1 for Lemon Squares

Parameter:
1. FiberUsage
[4, 6] # Units of fiber required for each bakery item
2. ChocolateMixUsage
5 # Units of chocolate mix required for a brownie
3. LemonMixUsage
7 # Units of lemon mix required for a lemon square
4. MaxChocolateMix
2500 # Units of chocolate mix available
5. MaxLemonMix
3300 # Units of lemon mix available
6. MinBrowniePercentage
0.4 # Minimum percentage of brownies among all items

Decision variable:
1. ItemCount
Integer variable, \( x_{i} \) for all \( i \in I \), representing the number of items of type \( i \) to be made.

Objective:
Minimize the total amount of fiber needed.
min: \( \sum_{i \in I} FiberUsage[i] \cdot x_{i} \)

Constraint:
1. Chocolate mix constraint
\( ChocolateMixUsage \cdot x_{0} \leq MaxChocolateMix \)
2. Lemon mix constraint
\( LemonMixUsage \cdot x_{1} \leq MaxLemonMix \)
3. Sales preference constraint
\( x_{1} \geq x_{0} + 1 \)
4. Brownie percentage constraint
\( x_{0} \geq MinBrowniePercentage \cdot (x_{0} + x_{1}) \) which simplifies to \( 3 \cdot x_{0} \geq 2 \cdot x_{1} \)
5. Non-negativity
\( x_{i} \geq 0 \), for all \( i \in I \)

Type:
Integer, linear, linear
