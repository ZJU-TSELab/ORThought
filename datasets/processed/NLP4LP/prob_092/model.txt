Set:
1. BoneType
The set of bone types, \( I = \{0, 1\} \) # 0 for small, 1 for large

Parameter:
1. MedicationUsage
[10, 15] # Units of tooth medication required for each bone type
2. MeatUsage
[12, 15] # Units of meat required for each bone type
3. MaxMedication
2000 # Maximum units of tooth medication available
4. MinLargeBones
30 # Minimum number of large bones to be made
5. MinSmallBonePercentage
0.5 # Minimum percentage of small bones

Decision variable:
1. BoneCount
Integer variable, \( x_{i} \) for all \( i \in I \), representing the number of bones of type \( i \) to be made.

Objective:
Minimize the total amount of meat needed.
min: \( \sum_{i \in I} MeatUsage[i] \cdot x_{i} \)

Constraint:
1. Tooth medication constraint
\( \sum_{i \in I} MedicationUsage[i] \cdot x_{i} \leq MaxMedication \)
2. Small bone percentage constraint
\( x_{0} \geq MinSmallBonePercentage \cdot (x_{0} + x_{1}) \) which simplifies to \( x_{0} \geq x_{1} \)
3. Minimum large bones constraint
\( x_{1} \geq MinLargeBones \)
4. Non-negativity
\( x_{i} \geq 0 \), for all \( i \in I \)

Type:
Integer, linear, linear
