Set:
1. SandwichType
The set of sandwich types, \( S = \{0, 1\} \) # 0 for Regular, 1 for Special

Parameter:
1. EggsAvailable
40 (Total eggs available)
2. BaconAvailable
70 (Total slices of bacon available)
3. Profit
[3, 4] # Profit per sandwich type: $3 for Regular, $4 for Special
4. EggsRequired
[2, 3] # Eggs required per sandwich type: 2 for Regular, 3 for Special
5. BaconRequired
[3, 5] # Bacon required per sandwich type: 3 for Regular, 5 for Special

Decision variable:
1. SandwichCount
Integer variable, \( x_{s} \) for all \( s \in S \), representing the number of sandwiches of type \( s \) to produce.

Objective:
Maximize the total profit from producing sandwiches.
max: \sum_{s \in S} Profit[s] * x_{s}

Constraint:
1. Eggs constraint
\sum_{s \in S} EggsRequired[s] * x_{s} \leq EggsAvailable
2. Bacon constraint
\sum_{s \in S} BaconRequired[s] * x_{s} \leq BaconAvailable
3. Non-negativity
x_{s} \geq 0, \forall s \in S

Type:
Integer, linear, linear
