Set:
1. ArtPieceType
The set of art piece types, \( T = \{0, 1\} \) # 0 for Large, 1 for Small

Parameter:
1. PaintAvailable
100 # Total units of paint available
2. GlitterAvailable
50 # Total units of glitter available
3. GlueAvailable
70 # Total units of glue available
4. MinArtPieces
[5, 5] # Minimum units of each art piece type required
5. Profit
[30, 15] # Profit per art piece type
6. PaintRequired
[4, 2] # Units of paint required per art piece type
7. GlitterRequired
[3, 1] # Units of glitter required per art piece type
8. GlueRequired
[5, 2] # Units of glue required per art piece type

Decision variable:
1. ArtPieceCount
Integer variable, \( x_{t} \) for all \( t \in T \), representing the number of art pieces of type \( t \) to make.

Objective:
Maximize the total profit.
max: \sum_{t \in T} Profit[t] * x_{t}

Constraint:
1. Paint constraint
\sum_{t \in T} PaintRequired[t] * x_{t} \leq PaintAvailable
2. Glitter constraint
\sum_{t \in T} GlitterRequired[t] * x_{t} \leq GlitterAvailable
3. Glue constraint
\sum_{t \in T} GlueRequired[t] * x_{t} \leq GlueAvailable
4. Minimum large art pieces
x_{t} \geq MinArtPieces[t], \forall t \in T
5. Non-negativity
x_{t} \geq 0, \forall t \in T

Type:
Integer, linear, linear
