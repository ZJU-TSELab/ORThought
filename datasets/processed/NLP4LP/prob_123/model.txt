Set:
1. CakeType
The set of cake types, \( I = \{0, 1\} \) # 0 for cheesecake, 1 for caramel cake

Parameter:
1. Calories
[200, 250] # Calories per slice for each cake type
2. Sugar
[40, 50] # Grams of sugar per slice for each cake type
3. MaxCalories
10000 # Maximum calories allowed
4. MinCaramelSlices
3 # Minimum number of caramel cake slices
5. CheesecakeToCaramelRatio
3 # Minimum ratio of cheesecake slices to caramel cake slices

Decision variable:
1. CakeSlices
Integer variable, \( x_{i} \) for all \( i \in I \), representing the number of slices of each cake type.

Objective:
Maximize the total amount of sugar consumed.
max: \( \sum_{i \in I} Sugar[i] \cdot x_{i} \)

Constraint:
1. Calorie constraint
\( \sum_{i \in I} Calories[i] \cdot x_{i} \leq MaxCalories \)
2. Minimum caramel slices constraint
\( x_{1} \geq MinCaramelSlices \)
3. Preference constraint
\( x_{0} \geq CheesecakeToCaramelRatio \cdot x_{1} \)
4. Non-negativity
\( x_{i} \geq 0 \), for all \( i \in I \)

Type:
Integer, linear, linear
